About Rabies

Critical information about rabies prevention, symptoms, and treatment

Important: Rabies is a serious viral disease that is almost always fatal once symptoms appear. Immediate medical attention is crucial after any animal bite.

What is Rabies?

Rabies is a viral disease that affects the central nervous system of mammals, including humans. The virus is typically transmitted through the saliva of infected animals via bites or scratches. Once clinical symptoms appear, rabies is almost invariably fatal, making prevention absolutely critical.

How Rabies Spreads

  • Animal Bites: The most common transmission route, especially from dogs, cats, bats, and wild animals
  • Scratches: Deep scratches from infected animals can transmit the virus
  • Open Wounds: Contact between infected saliva and open wounds or mucous membranes
  • Note: Rabies is not transmitted through touching or petting an infected animal, or through contact with their blood, urine, or feces

Symptoms in Animals

Early Signs

  • Behavioral changes
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fever
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Excessive drooling

Advanced Symptoms

  • Aggressive behavior
  • Paralysis
  • Seizures
  • Hydrophobia (fear of water)
  • Disorientation

Prevention Strategies

Prevention is the best protection against rabies!
  • Vaccinate Your Pets: Ensure all dogs and cats receive regular rabies vaccinations
  • Avoid Stray Animals: Don't approach or feed unknown animals, especially if they appear sick
  • Report Suspicious Animals: Contact authorities if you see animals behaving strangely
  • Educate Your Family: Teach children never to approach unknown animals
  • Secure Your Property: Keep pet food indoors and seal garbage to avoid attracting wild animals

What to Do After an Animal Bite

Emergency Action Required: Follow these steps immediately after any animal bite.
  1. Clean the Wound (First 15 minutes):
    • Wash thoroughly with soap and running water for at least 15 minutes
    • Apply antiseptic (iodine, alcohol, or hydrogen peroxide)
    • Do not close the wound with bandages initially
  2. Seek Immediate Medical Attention:
    • Go to the nearest hospital or clinic immediately
    • Contact your doctor or emergency services
    • Time is critical - do not delay
  3. Gather Information:
    • Try to identify the animal (species, size, color)
    • Note the animal's behavior and location
    • Take photos if safely possible
  4. Report the Incident:
    • Contact local animal control authorities
    • Report to police if necessary
    • Use our reporting system to log the incident

Post-Exposure Treatment

If you've been bitten by an animal that might have rabies, you will likely need:

  • Rabies Immune Globulin (RIG): Provides immediate protection
  • Rabies Vaccine Series: Usually 4-5 doses over 14-28 days
  • Wound Care: Professional cleaning and tetanus shot if needed
  • Follow-up Care: Monitor for any signs of infection

Rabies in India

India accounts for approximately 36% of global rabies deaths, with an estimated 18,000-20,000 fatalities annually. Key facts:

  • Dogs are responsible for 97% of human rabies cases in India
  • Children aged 5-15 are at highest risk
  • Rural areas have limited access to post-exposure treatment
  • Mass dog vaccination programs are the most effective prevention strategy
