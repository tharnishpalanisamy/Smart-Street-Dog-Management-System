Join Our Volunteer Team

Make a difference in the lives of street dogs and your community

Thank you for your interest in volunteering! Every helping hand makes a significant impact in our mission to create a safer, more humane environment for both dogs and humans.

Why Volunteer With Us?

  • Make a Real Impact: Directly contribute to reducing street dog populations and improving animal welfare
  • Learn New Skills: Gain experience in animal handling, veterinary care, and community outreach
  • Community Building: Connect with like-minded individuals who care about animal welfare
  • Flexible Commitment: Choose volunteer opportunities that fit your schedule and interests
  • Professional Development: Gain valuable experience for careers in veterinary science, social work, or animal care

Volunteer Opportunities

Field Operations

  • Assist in dog rescue operations
  • Support veterinary teams during ABC procedures
  • Help with post-operative care and monitoring
  • Transport dogs to and from treatment centers

Time Commitment: 4-8 hours per week

Community Outreach

  • Conduct awareness programs in schools and communities
  • Distribute educational materials
  • Organize vaccination camps
  • Train local community members

Time Commitment: 3-6 hours per week

Data Management

  • Update dog information in database
  • Process and verify reports
  • Maintain digital records
  • Generate statistical reports

Time Commitment: 2-4 hours per week

Administrative Support

  • Coordinate volunteer schedules
  • Manage supplies and equipment
  • Assist with fundraising events
  • Handle correspondence

Time Commitment: 3-5 hours per week

Emergency Response

  • Respond to urgent rescue calls
  • Provide first aid to injured dogs
  • Handle aggressive dog situations
  • Coordinate with veterinary services

Time Commitment: On-call basis

Feeding Programs

  • Organize regular feeding schedules
  • Prepare and distribute nutritious food
  • Monitor dog health during feeding
  • Maintain feeding location records

Time Commitment: 2-3 hours per week

Volunteer Requirements

General Requirements

  • Age: Minimum 18 years (16-17 with parental consent)
  • Health: Good physical health and current tetanus vaccination
  • Commitment: Minimum 3-month commitment preferred
  • Training: Complete mandatory orientation and safety training
  • Background Check: Basic verification for safety purposes

Specialized Roles

  • Field Operations: Experience with animals preferred, physical fitness required
  • Data Management: Basic computer skills, attention to detail
  • Community Outreach: Good communication skills, local language fluency
  • Emergency Response: Previous animal handling experience, availability for urgent calls

Training Program

All volunteers undergo comprehensive training to ensure safety and effectiveness:

  • Orientation Session (2 hours): Program overview, policies, and procedures
  • Animal Safety Training (4 hours): Safe animal handling, recognizing signs of illness, personal protection
  • First Aid Certification (6 hours): Basic first aid for both animals and humans
  • Role-Specific Training (2-8 hours): Detailed training for your chosen volunteer role
  • Ongoing Education: Monthly workshops and skill development sessions

Volunteer Benefits

  • Free training and certification in animal care
  • Transportation allowance for field work
  • Volunteer appreciation events and recognition
  • Letters of recommendation for employment or education
  • Priority access to veterinary care workshops
  • Annual volunteer appreciation awards
